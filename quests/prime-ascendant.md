## 2. Purpose and distinctions

Prime Ascendant exists to design, build, prove, and eventually govern a self-hosted Prime chapter in controlled phases. It remains separate from the earlier Prime reconstruction and host-substrate work:

| Work | Durable boundary |
|---|---|
| Prime Reborn | Repository reconstruction, parity, recovery, and cutover-readiness for Prime source. |
| Prometheus's Fire | Hardware, hypervisor, VM/LXC, storage, network, backup, restore, and host substrate. |
| Prime Ascendant | Application semantics, Living Memory, Nexus Core, Dawnshard, private control surface, future Gitea authority migration, local intelligence coordination, and workflow refraction. |

The realm names used by the roadmap are shorthand for bounded interfaces. They do not create new Projects, folders, vaults, evidence stores, or runtime authority.

## 3. Accepted principles

- GitHub `Jktomy/atlas-prime` remains the durable canonical source until an explicit future cutover.
- PostgreSQL provides future Atlas Living Memory operational state; it does not replace durable doctrine.
- Nexus Core is the authoritative application writer for governed Living Memory transitions.
- Models, browsers, and arbitrary workflows never receive unrestricted direct SQL authority.
- PostgreSQL full-text search plus `pgvector` is the initial retrieval direction. Qdrant remains deferred until demonstrated need.
- Dawnshard is Athena's restart-safe delta and reconciliation view, not a second source of truth.
- A private Tailscale-only Atlas website is the future local/mobile control surface; no public endpoint is authorized.
- Artemis and local models may assist without source, merge, infrastructure, or silent-action authority.
- Gitea may become canonical only after parity, recovery, mirror, rollback, mobile/Athena access, and Jayson-approved cutover.
- Legacy routes remain until parity, recovery, rollback, and explicit retirement approval are proven.
- Protected evidence remains in appropriate private systems, and secrets remain in a password manager rather than Git, Dawnshard, Living Memory exports, or model context.
- Preview -> Execute remains mandatory for durable, runtime, infrastructure, automation, migration, deletion, and source-truth actions.

## 4. Authority and non-runtime boundary

The Quest records clean doctrine, schemas, source routes, sanitized status, and future acceptance criteria. It does not claim that Living Memory, Nexus Core, Dawnshard, the private website, Gitea shadow, local intelligence, or any integrated service is deployed or proven. Runtime and infrastructure actions require their own source transaction, exact route, separate authority, and protected evidence. Canonical Gitea cutover remains a future Jayson gate.

## 5. Campaign roadmap

### PA-C01 — Write the Covenant

**State:** `ACTIVE — ARCHITECTURE REFINEMENT`  
**Purpose:** Define constitutional and authority boundaries; establish source/memory relationships and protected-data boundaries; identify unresolved decisions; define acceptance criteria; reconcile Prime Reborn and Prometheus's Fire; prevent premature implementation.  
**Terminal gate:** `COVENANT ACCEPTED / ARCHITECTURE BOUNDARIES EXPLICIT / NO RUNTIME AUTHORIZED`

PA-C01 is the current Campaign and is not complete.

### PA-C02 — Raise the Living Archive

Design and prove PostgreSQL Living Memory, including schemas, events, audit, retention, export, backup, restore, WAL, PITR, reconciliation, full-text search, and `pgvector`.

### PA-C03 — Forge Nexus Core

Define and implement Nexus Core as the governed application writer with validation, authorization, receipts, idempotency, failure behavior, rollback, and mediated integrations.

### PA-C04 — Shape the Dawnshard

Define Dawnshard schemas and generation, event watermarks, stale and fail-closed behavior, restart-safe outputs, integrity, and portable backup.

### PA-C05 — Open the Private Gate

Build the private Atlas website/API with approved private access, authentication, approvals, health, receipts, read views, and bounded actions. No public endpoint.

### PA-C06 — Crown Gitea

Establish Gitea in shadow mode and prove parity, recovery, mirror, rollback, and access. Canonical cutover remains a separate Jayson-controlled gate.

### PA-C07 — Awaken Artemis

Connect local intelligence safely for bounded retrieval, embeddings, reranking, OCR, and assistance while preserving mediated authority and measuring resource cost.

### PA-C08 — Bind the Realms

Integrate Prime source, Living Memory, Dawnshard, Nexus Core, private website, Gitea shadow, Artemis, Prometheus, Forge, Apollo, and Notum. Define degraded modes and prove monitoring remains nonauthoritative.

### PA-C09 — Temper the Old Blades

Compare the new workflows with Spear, Arrow/Bow, Sword/Oathbringer, Phoenix Blade, Aegis Break, Thread Engine, Codex, and GitHub routes. Prove parity and rollback before proposing retention, simplification, supersession, or retirement. No route is retired during Quest creation.

### PA-C10 — Prove the Dawn

Perform integrated acceptance for backup, restore, restart, rollback, reconciliation, stale-state handling, private access, model boundaries, cutover readiness, full Strikeforce, and restart-safe Sunset. The Quest may not complete merely because services are running.
